Understanding child malnutrition

Malnutrition is not just about “too little food” — it is a complex condition that affects growth, learning and immunity. Early detection and counselling can save lives.

Causes, symptoms & prevention

Use this visual guide during counselling sessions with parents and caregivers.

🌾
Main causes
  • Inadequate breastfeeding and complementary feeding practices.
  • Repeated infections such as diarrhoea, pneumonia and worm infestations.
  • Poverty, food insecurity and limited access to diverse foods.
🩺
Common symptoms
  • Low weight for age or height, visible thinness.
  • Swelling of feet, hair changes, frequent illness.
  • Child appears tired, irritable or less active than peers.
🛡
Prevention steps
  • Exclusive breastfeeding for 6 months and continued breastfeeding up to 2 years.
  • Timely introduction of diverse, nutrient-dense complementary foods.
  • Regular growth monitoring, deworming and vitamin A supplementation.
